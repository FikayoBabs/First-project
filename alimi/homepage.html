Mentorship is the guidance provided by a mentor, especially an experienced person in a company or educational institution.Mentorship is a relationship in which a more experienced or more knowledgeable person helps to guide a less experienced or less knowledgeable person. The mentor may be older or younger than the person being mentored, but he or she must have a certain area of expertise.

What do mentors do?

  • Teaches the mentee about a specific issue.
  • They coach the mentee on a particular skill
  • They Facilitate the mentee's growth by sharing resources and networks
  • They Challenge the mentee to move beyond his or her comfort
  • They Create a safe learning environment for taking risks
  • They Focus on the mentee's total development

Benefits of being a mentor

  • Enhancing strategic business initiatives
  • Encouraging retention
  • Reducing turnover costs
  • Improving productivity
  • Breaking down the "silo" mentality that hinders cooperation among company departments or divisions.
  • Elevating knowledge transfer from just getting information and to retaining the practical experience and wisdom gained from long-term employees.
  • Enhancing professional development.

Things a mentee can gain from a mentor

  • Receive assistance with long-term career planning and direction.
  • Receive help with clarifying career goals and course of action.
  • Receive good advice and practical help for obtaining additional training.
  • Receive general constructive criticism and guidance.
